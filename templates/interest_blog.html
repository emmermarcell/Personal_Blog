Book Recommendations

  • The Stranger by Albert Camus
    A philosophical novel that explores the themes of existentialism and absurdism, following the story of a man named Meursault who kills an Arab and faces the consequences.
  • Dune by Frank Herbert
    A groundbreaking sci-fi saga by Frank Herbert. Set in a distant future, it follows noble houses, factions, and messianic figures on the desert planet Arrakis, known as Dune. With its rich world-building and complex characters, the series delves into power struggles, prophetic visions, and ecological complexities.
  • Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman! by Richard Feynman
    An autobiographical book by the physicist Richard Feynman, which describes his life and work in an entertaining and humorous way.
  • The Feynman Lectures on Physics by Richard Feynman, Robert B. Leighton, and Matthew Sands
    A set of lectures by Richard Feynman, which provide an introduction to the field of physics and cover a wide range of topics, from mechanics to electromagnetism to quantum mechanics.
  • Physics and Beyond by Werner Heisenberg
    A book by the Nobel Prize-winning physicist Werner Heisenberg, which explores the philosophical implications of quantum mechanics and the role of science in society.
